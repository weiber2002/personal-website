Life Outside the Lab

Beyond Silicon,
Still Going Hard.

Designing chips and staying active share more than you'd think — both demand meticulous preparation, the ability to push through bottlenecks, and knowing when to pace yourself. Here's what I do when I'm not staring at waveforms.

01

NTU EE Volleyball

I play volleyball five days a week with the NTU EE department team (NTUEE) — competing in inter-department tournaments and genuinely loving every training session with the team.

There's something irreplaceable about showing up to practice, grinding through drills, and then pulling off a play you've been working on for weeks. Winning a tournament trophy with this group of people is one of the things I'm most proud of from my time at NTU.

02

Triathlon Finisher

51.5 km Olympic Distance
Swim · Bike · Run 3 disciplines, 1 race
Finisher ✓ No DNF. Ever.

There's a particular kind of stubbornness required to sign up for a triathlon. You swim until your arms give out, clip into a bike for 40 km under the Taiwan sun, then somehow convince your legs to run a 10k when they've already checked out.

The run was where it got ugly. Around kilometer 6, the cramps hit — both legs, simultaneously, the kind that make you question every decision you've ever made. I didn't stop. I hobbled, skipped, grimaced, and shuffled my way through the last 4k in whatever motion could loosely be called running. Crossing that finish line felt less like an achievement and more like a negotiation with my own body — and I won, barely.

Bib #1088, transition zone, right before the bike leg — still smiling because the cramps hadn't started yet. The mountain shot is from a training ride up to the reservoir, one of those climbs where the view on the way down makes you forget how much it hurt going up. And yes, I'm still wearing my glasses on the run. Some things never change.
